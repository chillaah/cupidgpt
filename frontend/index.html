FAQ

Why is the study being conducted?

This research project is being undertaken as part of a study by Dr. Alan Woodley and Chiran Walisundara.
Imagine you are on a TV dating show. You stand on one side of a curtain and ask possible suitors some questions.
After hearing their answers, you have to choose which suitor you'd like to go on a date with.
However, one of the suitors is an AI machine. Do you think you could tell the difference?
The purpose of this project is to see how many people can distinguish between a human and a machine.
 

What does participation involve?

There are two ways to participate:
1. Answer typical ‘first-date’ type questions, like “Describe your idea of a perfect date.”
2. Read two prepared answers for a single question and compare them. Decide which answer is the most romantic or fun and identify which was written by a human or AI.
Your participation is entirely voluntary. You can withdraw at any time without penalty.
 

What are the possible benefits for me if I take part?

This research is unlikely to benefit you directly.
However, it may benefit society by highlighting if people are susceptible to romance scams.
 

What are the possible risks for me if I take part?

There are no risks beyond normal day-to-day living associated with this project.
 

What about privacy and confidentiality?

The research team will record your gender and age bracket. Personal identifying information will not be collected.
Data will be securely stored as per QUT policies and kept for a minimum of 5 years.
